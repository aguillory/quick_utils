ðŸ“‹ Quick Instructions (Calcium Chloride)

  • Measure your initial liquid (and lemon juice, if using).
  • Use the calculated amount of sugar from "Sugar to Mix w/ Pectin" and whisk thoroughly with the Total Pectin.
  • Gently warm the main liquid. Disperse the pectin/sugar mix into the warm (not boiling) liquid while whisking vigorously to prevent clumps.
  • Bring the mixture to a full rolling boil. Boil for 1 minute to fully hydrate the pectin.
  • Add the "Remaining Sugar" and any other solids. Return to a boil.
  • Remove from heat. Add any final acids (like the lemon juice if you didn't add it initially).
  • Prepare your calcium solution: Mix the Calcium Chloride Powder into the "Liquid for Ca Solution".
  • Briskly stir the calcium solution into the final hot mixture. It will begin to set quickly. Pour into containers immediately.

ðŸ“‹ Quick Instructions (Calcium Sulfate)

  • Measure your initial liquid (and lemon juice, if using).
  • In a dry bowl, thoroughly whisk together the Total Pectin, the Calcium Sulfate Powder, and the sugar from "Sugar to Mix w/ Pectin". This dry mix is critical.
  • Gently warm the main liquid. Disperse the dry mix into the warm (not boiling) liquid while whisking vigorously to prevent clumps.
  • Bring the mixture to a full rolling boil. Boil for 1 minute to fully hydrate the pectin.
  • Add the "Remaining Sugar" and any other solids. Return to a boil.
  • Remove from heat. Add any final acids. The gel will begin to set as it cools. Pour into containers.

ðŸ’¡ Notes & Tips

  • Tap water contains calcium! If your tap water is hard, you may need less added calcium than calculated. For precise results, consider using distilled water if making a calcium chloride solution.
  • The formula for total weight is $TotalWeight = \frac{LiquidWeight}{1 - (PectinFraction + BrixFraction)}$. Your recipe will fail if Pectin % + Brix % is 100% or more.
  • The sugar-to-pectin mix is crucial for preventing clumps. This calculator uses a 4:1 ratio.
